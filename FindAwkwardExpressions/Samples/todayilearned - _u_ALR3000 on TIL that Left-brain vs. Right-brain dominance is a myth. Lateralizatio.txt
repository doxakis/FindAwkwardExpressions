/u/ALR3000 on TIL that Left-brain vs. Right-brain dominance is a myth. Lateralization is unique in every individual and researchers can not find any evidence that one side of the brain or another works by itself for functions of our personalities.
The term is usually used to refer to language and motor dominance. Most people have primary language centers on the left (even most left handers) and clearly most people have one dominant motor hemisphere--and are right handed or otherwise. Right hemisphere does handle music and language intonation more than the left (e.g. development of aprosodia after strokes on the right).
